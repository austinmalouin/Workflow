# RIDGEWOOD TikTok Organic Content Plan — Weeks 1-2

**Date drafted**: 2026-07-23
**Context**: Part of the approved organic-first traffic plan (see [traffic-options.md](traffic-options.md)).
$0 spend, TikTok sales channel already enabled in Shopify admin but unused.
**Constraint this plan is built around**: this is a dropship catalog (StorePilot) with **no original
photography or video yet** — every concept below is chosen specifically to work without filming a
studio-quality product shoot, using supplier stock images/video, screen-recorded product pages,
text-and-voiceover list formats, or Austin filming himself with the physical product in hand once
a sample arrives. Concepts requiring in-hand demo footage are marked accordingly.

## Before posting: 3 setup items (not content, but required first)

1. **Claim/verify the TikTok Business account** tied to the already-enabled TikTok sales channel and
   turn on the TikTok Shop / product-tagging feature so videos can tag actual product links —
   otherwise organic views can't convert to site traffic at all. This is an account-settings action,
   not a content one — flag for Austin to do directly (falls under "connect a new
   integration" in CLAUDE.md's hard constraints).
2. **Order 2-4 physical samples** of the highest-potential products (recommend: TurboBeam 2000
   Flashlight, Telescopic Camping Lantern, Emergency 7-in-1 Whistle, RainGuard Backpack Cover — cheap,
   compact, and each has a clear single-feature demo) so at least some videos can use real in-hand
   footage instead of only stock/screen content. This is a real-money purchase decision for Austin,
   not something to action here.
3. **Bio + link setup**: TikTok bio link to ridgewoodequipment.store (or a specific collection page),
   consistent handle/branding across TikTok and the Shopify store name.

## Content pillars (mix across the 2 weeks)

- **List-format / "gear you didn't know existed"** — voiceover + on-screen text over product stock
  photos/video clips, no filming required. Lowest production cost, highest volume potential.
- **Screen-recorded product-page walkthroughs** — literally screen-record scrolling a product page
  with voiceover, styled as "found this on a random site and it's actually good" — works entirely
  from existing (soon-to-be-rewritten) product pages, zero new assets needed.
- **UGC-style demo** (once a sample arrives) — Austin filming himself actually using the item.
  Lowest volume but highest trust; save for products with the clearest single demo-able feature.
- **"10 essentials" / preparedness framing** — ties several small SKUs (whistle, compass, keychain,
  carabiner) into one video instead of one video per tiny item, which is a better use of production
  time for low-ticket accessories.

## 2-week plan: 7 video concepts

### Video 1 (Day 1-2) — "5 camping essentials under $25" [list format, stock footage]
Fast-cut list video: Emergency 7-in-1 Whistle, Portable Survival Keychain Compass, Military Paracord
Keychain, Adventure Survival Carabiner Kit, RainGuard Backpack Cover. Voiceover names one real
feature per item (compass, bottle opener, magnetic clip, etc. — pulled from the actual rewritten
product copy, not invented). On-screen price overlay per item. CTA: "link in bio."
*No filming required — supplier images/stock clips only.*

### Video 2 (Day 3-4) — "POV: your flashlight actually has 2000 lumens" [demo, once sample arrives]
Simple in-hand demo: TurboBeam 2000 Flashlight in a dark room/backyard at night, showing beam
distance and the 3 modes. This is the single easiest "wow" demo in the catalog — bright-light demos
reliably perform well on TikTok because the visual payoff needs no explanation.
*Requires the physical sample — highest priority item to order first.*

### Video 3 (Day 5-6) — "I screen-recorded this backpack's actual page and I have questions" [screen-record, no filming]
Screen-recording of the (rewritten) 75L Adventure Rucksack product page, voiceover reacting to the
rain-cover-in-a-zipper-pocket feature and the hydration port, styled as a genuine reaction rather
than an ad. This format works specifically because the *rewritten* copy will finally have real,
specific features worth reacting to — sequence this video after the SEO rewrite goes live.
*No filming required.*

### Video 4 (Day 7-8) — "Testing the magnetic camp light in weird places" [demo, once sample arrives]
In-hand demo of the HOTO Waterproof Rechargeable Camp Light's magnetic base — stick it to a fridge,
a car door, a metal shelf, showing the "sticks anywhere" hook rather than a generic unboxing.
Magnetic-mount demos are a proven TikTok format (satisfying "click" + immediate visual payoff).
*Requires the physical sample.*

### Video 5 (Day 9-10) — "Ranking every light in our camping light lineup" [comparison list, stock + demo mix]
Side-by-side comparison of the 3 overlapping lighting SKUs (TurboBeam Flashlight, LED Adventure
Light, HOTO Camp Light, Telescopic Lantern) — lumens, modes, and best-use-case for each, styled as
a genuine buyer's-guide rather than a sales pitch. Doubles as real differentiation content since the
SEO rewrite already had to solve "why would someone pick this one over the other two."
*Mix of stock footage and demo footage if samples have arrived by Day 9.*

### Video 6 (Day 11-12) — "The 7-in-1 whistle has how many tools in it?" [demo/close-up, once sample arrives]
Close-up demo unfolding/using each of the 7 functions on the Emergency 7-in-1 Adventure Whistle
(compass, thermometer, signal light, magnifying glass, etc.) one at a time — a natural "wait, it does
WHAT" hook format that doesn't require any location beyond a table and good lighting.
*Requires the physical sample.*

### Video 7 (Day 13-14) — "Would you actually survive with $80 of gear from this site" [challenge/story format, stock + demo mix]
Loose narrative format: picks ~4-5 items across the catalog (rucksack, whistle, flashlight, carabiner
kit) and narrates a hypothetical "day hike went wrong" scenario using them, half stock/half demo
footage depending on what's arrived. Story format tends to outperform straight product lists for
reach, and doubles as a soft way to show the catalog's actual breadth.
*Mix of stock and demo footage.*

## Posting cadence and expectations (grounded, not hype)

Per [traffic-options.md](traffic-options.md)'s research: reliable, repeatable organic TikTok traffic
for an unknown brand realistically takes **8-16 weeks of consistent posting** before a discernible
pattern emerges, and a single viral video is a high-variance lottery outcome, not something to plan
around. This 2-week, 7-video plan is the *start* of that runway, not a complete campaign — the
realistic next step after these 7 is to keep posting at 3-4x/week using the same pillars (rotate
through the remaining catalog: mosquito hammock, sleeping bag poncho, hiking poles, dining set, etc.)
rather than treating this as a one-time push.

## What this plan deliberately does NOT include

- No claims of certifications, ratings, or "best-selling" status not verifiable from the supplier.
- No use of the fake per-product review counts currently live on the site (flagged separately in
  BUSINESS.md as a real FTC-risk issue, same category as the sitewide testimonials section already
  removed) — video scripts should not reference "hundreds of 5-star reviews" since that's not true.
- No paid boosting or ad spend — this is the organic-only half of the approved plan.
- No axe/knife SKUs — while TikTok's organic weapons policy is less strict than its ads policy,
  keeping the content pillar entirely on non-weapon gear avoids any account-flag risk before the
  channel has any track record to fall back on.
