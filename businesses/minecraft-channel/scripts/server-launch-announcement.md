# Episode 0 — "A Throne With No King" (Server Launch / Announcement Video)

Series: Houses of the Throne | Format: cinematic documentary-style narration over gameplay/build
footage | Est. runtime: 6-9 minutes | Voice: voiceover (face-optional)

Purpose: this is the funnel's first touchpoint. Job #1 is making a stranger want to open Minecraft
and type in a server IP by the end of the video. Everything else is secondary.

---

## Hook (0:00-0:15) — do not skip pacing on this, it decides click-through/retention

Cold open, no intro/logo yet. Cinematic slow pan across an empty, unclaimed landscape at dawn —
plains, a river, distant mountains, one crumbling ruined watchtower. Music: low, tense, a single
horn note.

**VO:** "Right now, this land belongs to no one. In a week, it will belong to whoever was bold
enough to claim it first — and ruthless enough to keep it."

Cut to a fast 2-second flash-forward montage (teaser of what's coming later in the series): a
banner being raised, a siege ladder hitting a wall, two players shaking hands over a trade,
a throne room reveal. Then hard cut to black, title card: **HOUSES OF THE THRONE**.

## Setup (0:15-1:30) — what this world is

VO over slow flythrough of the map's key regions (river crossing, forest, coastline, mountain
pass — whatever the actual built/generated map has):

- Establish premise in plain terms, no lore-dump: this is a real Minecraft server. Anyone can
  join. On day one, the land is empty. Players found Houses, swear banners, claim territory, and
  build up a seat of power — and at the end of each season, the Houses that hold the most
  territory (or win it in open war) control "the Throne" — a real in-game holding with real
  seasonal perks for whoever controls it.
- Name-check the genre honestly, once, for search/discovery and audience self-selection: "if you
  like Game of Thrones, this is the closest thing to actually playing it" — said as a comparison,
  never as a claim of official affiliation.
- One sentence on stakes: Houses can ally, trade, betray, or go to war — war happens in scheduled,
  announced windows, not random griefing, so it stays fair for smaller Houses.

## Rules of the game (1:30-3:00) — the part that makes people trust it's fair

Show, don't just tell — cut to UI/menu footage (claim map, House creation screen, Throne-holding
scoreboard) while VO explains:

- How to found or join a House (in-game command + Discord role)
- How land claiming works and its limits (so new players know a mega-House can't just wall off
  the map)
- What "the Throne" mechanic actually rewards (be concrete: e.g. a controlling House gets a
  cosmetic seasonal banner in spawn, a shared perk, bragging rights — not a pay-to-win mechanic)
- One clear line on monetization, stated up front so it isn't a bait-and-switch later: "everything
  you can buy is cosmetic — nothing here is pay-to-win, ever." (This is both an EULA-compliance
  statement and a trust/retention signal — state it plainly, don't bury it.)

## Proof of stakes (3:00-5:00) — borrow credibility from the genre, then differentiate

Show a couple of quick clips/screenshots (if available) of impressive medieval Minecraft builds
(the server's own spawn/castle build, or concept art if the server isn't built out yet) to prove
this isn't a bare vanilla world — there's real production value going into the world itself.

VO acknowledges the genre honestly instead of oversell: "Kingdom servers aren't new — some of the
best Minecraft content ever made has been people building empires and going to war over them.
What's different here is [pick the true differentiator once locked: e.g. "every House is founded
by a real player, not a pre-picked cast" / "the whole story gets told, episode by episode, as it
actually happens"]."

## Call to action (5:00-end)

- Show the server IP on screen clearly, held long enough to screenshot, repeated in VO once.
- Tell viewers exactly what happens next: "Episode 1 goes up once the first Houses are founded —
  if you want to be one of the founders instead of watching it happen, this is the moment to
  join."
- End card: subscribe prompt, Discord invite (support/community hub), server IP one more time.
